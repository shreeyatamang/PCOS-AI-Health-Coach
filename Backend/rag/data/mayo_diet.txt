Source: Mayo Clinic
Category: Diet

PCOS-friendly diet focuses on improving insulin sensitivity and hormone balance.

Key Principles:
- Weight management: Losing even 5% of body weight can improve symptoms and fertility
- Choose low glycemic index (GI) carbohydrates such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes
- Eat high-fiber foods to slow digestion and regulate blood sugar
- Include lean protein such as chicken, fish, eggs, tofu, and low-fat dairy
- Consume healthy fats like olive oil, nuts, seeds, and fatty fish

Recommended Diet:
- Mediterranean diet is beneficial for reducing inflammation and improving metabolic health
- Low-GI diet helps control blood sugar and insulin levels

Lifestyle:
- At least 150 minutes of exercise per week improves insulin sensitivity
- Stress management supports hormone balance

Foods to Avoid:
- Sugary drinks
- Refined carbohydrates (white bread, pastries)
- Processed and fried foods
