Source: NHS
Category: Diet & Insulin Resistance

PCOS is associated with insulin resistance, which increases insulin levels and worsens symptoms.

Common Symptoms:
- Weight gain
- Irregular periods
- Acne
- Fatigue

Key Dietary Advice:

Carbohydrates:
- Control portion sizes (1–3 portions per meal)
- Reduce refined carbs and sugar

Low Glycemic Index Foods:
- Whole grains, oats, beans, lentils, sweet potatoes
- These help stabilize blood sugar and reduce symptoms

High GI Foods (limit):
- White bread, sugary drinks, processed foods

Diet Tips:
- Add protein or healthy fat to meals to slow glucose absorption
- Include vegetables and fiber-rich foods
- Eat fruits in moderation (berries are best)

Nutrients:
- Calcium: dairy or fortified alternatives
- Vitamin D: sunlight exposure or supplements
- Iron: lean meat, leafy greens
- Omega-3: fish, nuts, seeds

Lifestyle:
- Exercise improves insulin resistance
- Sleep and stress management are critical
