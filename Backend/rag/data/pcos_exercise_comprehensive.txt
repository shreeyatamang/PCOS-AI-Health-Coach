PCOS AND EXERCISE - COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE

Overview:
Physical activity is a first-line treatment for PCOS. Exercise improves insulin sensitivity, supports weight management, balances hormones, boosts mood, and improves overall quality of life. Regular exercise is recommended for ALL women with PCOS, regardless of weight.

Why Exercise Helps PCOS:
- Improves insulin sensitivity and glucose metabolism
- Reduces circulating androgen (male hormone) levels
- Supports healthy weight and body composition
- Improves cardiovascular fitness
- Regulates menstrual cycles
- Reduces depression and anxiety
- Improves sleep quality
- Boosts self-esteem and body image
- Reduces central (abdominal) fat, which is especially harmful in PCOS

How Much Exercise is Recommended:
- At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week (international guideline)
- Or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week
- Plus strength/resistance training 2-3 times per week
- Daily movement (even walking) provides meaningful benefit

Types of Exercise and Their Benefits:

1. Aerobic Exercise (Cardio)
- Includes: brisk walking, jogging, cycling, swimming, dancing, aerobics classes
- Improves cardiovascular fitness, burns calories, reduces insulin resistance
- Moderate intensity (where you can talk but are slightly breathless) is very effective
- Vigorous aerobic exercise shows stronger improvements in body composition and cardiorespiratory fitness

2. High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)
- Alternates between intense bursts of activity and rest periods
- Very effective for improving insulin sensitivity and reducing abdominal fat
- A 2023 study found HIIT for 3 months reduced depression and anxiety in women with PCOS
- More time-efficient than steady-state cardio
- Example: 20-30 seconds sprint, 40-60 seconds rest, repeat for 20 minutes

3. Resistance / Strength Training
- Builds lean muscle mass, which increases resting metabolic rate
- Improves insulin sensitivity and blood sugar control
- May reduce androgen levels (though evidence is still emerging)
- Includes: weight lifting, resistance bands, bodyweight exercises (squats, lunges, push-ups)
- Recommended 2-3 times per week on non-consecutive days

4. Yoga and Mind-Body Exercise
- Reduces cortisol (stress hormone) which worsens PCOS symptoms
- Improves mood, reduces anxiety and depression
- Supports hormonal balance through stress reduction
- Can be combined with other exercise forms
- Particularly helpful for stress-related PCOS flares

5. Walking
- The most accessible form of exercise
- Even 30 minutes of brisk walking daily improves insulin sensitivity
- A great starting point for beginners
- Can be broken into 10-minute segments throughout the day

Exercise Tips Specific to PCOS:

Getting Started:
- Start with low-to-moderate intensity and gradually increase
- Consistency is more important than intensity, especially at the beginning
- Choose activities you enjoy so you are more likely to stick with them
- Group classes or workout partners improve accountability

Overcoming Barriers:
- Women with PCOS tend to have lower fitness levels than women without PCOS
- Fatigue is common in PCOS - start with shorter sessions (15-20 minutes)
- Track progress to stay motivated (steps, distance, weight lifted)
- Rest days are important to avoid overtraining and injury

Combining Exercise Types:
- The most effective approach combines aerobic + resistance training
- This combination improves body composition, metabolic health, and hormone levels
- A sample weekly plan:
  - Monday: 30 min brisk walk + 20 min strength training
  - Wednesday: HIIT session (20-25 min)
  - Friday: 30 min cycling + 20 min strength training
  - Sunday: Yoga or gentle stretching

Exercise and Menstrual Cycle:
- Moderate exercise can help regulate irregular periods
- A 2023 systematic review found moderate-intensity exercise improved menstrual and reproductive health
- Excessive high-intensity exercise without adequate nutrition can worsen hormonal disruption
- Listen to your body - gentle movement during painful periods is fine

Exercise and Fertility:
- Regular physical activity supports ovulation and reproductive outcomes
- Combined with healthy diet, exercise can significantly improve fertility in women with PCOS
- Exercise alone or combined with diet and behavioral strategies improves metabolic health

Exercise and Mental Health:
- Exercise is one of the most effective natural antidepressants
- HIIT specifically has been shown to reduce depression and anxiety in PCOS
- Regular movement boosts endorphins, serotonin, and dopamine
- Exercise improves sleep quality, which further supports mood and hormone balance

Important Notes:
- No single exercise type is superior for all women with PCOS
- Personalize your routine based on your fitness level, preferences, and symptoms
- Exercise in conjunction with diet changes produces the best outcomes
- Always consult your doctor before starting a new exercise program if you have other health conditions
